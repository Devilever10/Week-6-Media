Kalye

Kalye (Tagalog for Street) is an upcoming animated film that examines the interactions that take place within the Filipino Street and Cityscape.

Much of what occurs in Manila, the major capital of the Philippines happens in the open. Children play basketball, there are old men sleeping on the sidewalk, prostitutes buy street food from stalls, you hear fireworks that are actually gunshots.

This film seeks to depict a nuanced and accurate depiction of both the dirtyness and beauty that exits within this setting. To shine a light on humanity, both good and bad.

As a result of the gradually increasing political/social & humanitarian situation in the Philippines, because of our infamous current President, Rodrigo Duterte. Many cameras turn to the country with a political agenda in mind. Just recently, a documentary called Di Ka Pasisiil won the Golden Dolphin award in the Cannes Film Festival
